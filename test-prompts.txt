TASK 1 — WRITING / CREATION
"Write Something Useful"
================================================================================

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Example A  |  Polite Email (professional writing)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SLM PATH — Goal Wizard inputs:
  One-sentence goal:
    Write a short, polite email to my professor asking for a 2-day extension on
    an upcoming homework assignment. Keep it professional and brief.

  Reframe (optional, pick one):
    "Refine my goal so the model knows exactly what to produce."

  Audience:   For a general audience
  Output format: Paragraph

  Constraints (add each one):
    - polite and professional tone
    - no more than 4 sentences
    - do not mention personal reasons

  Success criteria (add each one):
    - Sounds like a real student wrote it
    - Ends with a clear request

  → Click "Save intent"
  → Review the compiled prompt preview (accordion opens automatically)
  → Optionally click "Generate variants" to compare strict / shorter / explicit versions

SLM PATH — Chat message (type in composer, click Send):
  Please write the email.

  Follow-up options:
    Make it slightly more formal.
    Add a brief mention that I will submit by Friday.
    Shorten it to two sentences.

LLM PATH — Chat message (paste directly, click Send):
  Write a short, polite email to my professor asking for a 2-day extension on a
  homework assignment. Keep it professional, no more than 4 sentences, and do
  not mention personal reasons. End with a clear request.

  Follow-up options:
    Make it slightly more formal.
    Add a line saying I will submit by Friday evening.
    Give me two alternative versions — one formal, one casual.

TASK 2 — INFORMATION SEARCH / SUMMARIZATION
"Summarize Information"
================================================================================

Three passages are provided below. Copy the passage, paste it after your
chat message, and ask for the kind of summary you want.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Passage 1  |  Solar Energy (science / current events)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

--- PASSAGE START ---
Solar power installations worldwide surpassed 1 terawatt of total capacity in
2022, a milestone that analysts say marks the beginning of solar energy's
transition from niche technology to mainstream power source. The rapid growth
has been driven by falling manufacturing costs, which have dropped by roughly
90 percent over the past decade, making solar panels increasingly competitive
with fossil fuels even without government subsidies. In many regions of the
world, building new solar capacity is now cheaper than operating existing coal
or natural gas plants. Despite this progress, grid integration remains a
significant challenge: because solar panels only generate electricity when the
sun is shining, large-scale deployment requires investment in energy storage
systems and updated transmission infrastructure. Several countries are
addressing this by pairing solar farms with lithium-ion battery storage, though
critics note that scaling battery production brings its own resource and
environmental challenges.
--- PASSAGE END ---

SLM PATH — Goal Wizard inputs:
  One-sentence goal:
    Summarize a piece of text I provide and pull out the key points so I can
    understand it quickly without reading the whole thing. Give the result as
    bullet points.

  Audience:   Just for me
  Output format: Checklist

  Constraints:
    - 4–6 bullet points maximum
    - plain language, no jargon
    - include one bullet on the main challenge mentioned

  Success criteria:
    - I could explain the article to someone else using only the bullets
    - No bullet is longer than 20 words

SLM PATH — Chat message (paste passage after the instruction):
  Please summarize the following passage:

  [paste Passage 1 here]

  Follow-up options:
    Now give me one sentence I could use as a headline for this article.
    What is the single most important fact from this passage?
    Rewrite bullet 3 in even simpler language.

LLM PATH — Chat message (paste passage inline):
  Summarize the following passage in 4–6 bullet points. Use plain language.
  Include one bullet specifically about the main challenge described.

  [paste Passage 1 here]

  Follow-up options:
    Now give me a one-sentence headline for this passage.
    Explain the battery challenge in one simple sentence a non-expert would understand.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Passage 2  |  Home Network Setup (instructions / how-to)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

--- PASSAGE START ---
Setting up a home network begins with choosing a router that matches the size
of your space and the number of devices you expect to connect. For most homes,
a dual-band router operating on both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz frequencies provides a
good balance of range and speed. Once you have the router, connect it to your
modem using an Ethernet cable, then power on both devices. Use a laptop or
smartphone to connect to the router's default Wi-Fi network, whose name and
password are usually printed on a sticker on the router itself. Open a browser
and navigate to the router's administration page — commonly at 192.168.1.1 or
192.168.0.1 — to change the default Wi-Fi name and password to something more
secure. For larger homes or areas with weak signal, consider adding a mesh
network node or a Wi-Fi extender in a central location to eliminate dead spots.
--- PASSAGE END ---

SLM PATH — Goal Wizard inputs:
  One-sentence goal:
    Turn a set of technical instructions into a simple numbered checklist I
    can follow step by step without any background knowledge.

  Audience:   For a general audience
  Output format: Checklist

  Constraints:
    - numbered steps, not bullet points
    - no technical jargon — explain any terms in plain words
    - each step is one action only

  Success criteria:
    - Someone with no tech background could follow this
    - Steps are in the right order

SLM PATH — Chat message:
  Please convert the following instructions into a simple numbered checklist:

  [paste Passage 2 here]

  Follow-up options:
    Add a short note after step 4 explaining why changing the default password matters.
    Can you combine any steps that naturally go together?
    Give me a version with 5 steps max.

LLM PATH — Chat message:
  Convert the following instructions into a simple numbered checklist. Use plain
  language — no technical jargon. Each step should be one action only. Someone
  with no tech background should be able to follow it.

  [paste Passage 2 here]

Passage 3  |  Urban Farm (news-style / community interest)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

--- PASSAGE START ---
A community garden in Detroit has transformed a formerly vacant lot into a
thriving urban farm that supplies fresh vegetables to roughly 150 local
households each week. The project, started four years ago by a group of
neighbors frustrated by the lack of affordable produce nearby, now employs
six part-time workers and has expanded from a quarter-acre plot to nearly two
full acres. Volunteers tend the garden on weekends, and the organizers have
partnered with a local school to run an after-school program teaching children
basic horticulture and nutrition. The farm primarily grows leafy greens,
tomatoes, peppers, and herbs suited to Michigan's climate, and distributes its
harvest through a sliding-scale membership that allows low-income families to
pay less. The initiative has drawn interest from city planners looking to
replicate the model in other underserved neighborhoods, though funding remains
an ongoing challenge.

    - lead with the single most important fact
    - include who started it and why

  Success criteria:

    - Each bullet adds new information

TASK 3 — ADVICE / PLANNING
"Make a Plan"
================================================================================

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Example A  |  Study Schedule (deadline-driven)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SLM PATH — Goal Wizard inputs:
  One-sentence goal:
    Help me make a study schedule for a midterm exam in 5 days. I have about
    2 hours free each day and need to cover 4 chapters. Give me a day-by-day plan.

  Audience:   Just for me
  Output format: Checklist

  Constraints:
    - 2 hours maximum per day
    - leave Day 5 for review only, no new material
    - include one short break per session

  Success criteria:
    - Covers all 4 chapters before Day 5
    - Feels realistic, not overwhelming

SLM PATH — Chat message:
  Please make the study plan.

  Follow-up options:
    I only have 90 minutes on Day 2. Can you adjust?
    Add a note about what to do if I fall behind.
    Give me a version where I start with the hardest chapter first.

LLM PATH — Chat message:
  Help me make a 5-day study schedule for a midterm exam. I have 2 hours free
  each day and need to cover 4 chapters. Keep Day 5 for review only. Include a
  short break in each session. Make it feel realistic, not overwhelming.

  Follow-up options:
    Adjust Day 2 to 90 minutes because I have a commitment.
    What should I do if I fall behind on Day 3?

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Example B  |  Daily Task Schedule (time-boxing)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SLM PATH — Goal Wizard inputs:
  One-sentence goal:
    I have three work tasks, grocery shopping, and a gym session to fit into
    today. Help me schedule them so I finish everything before 8 PM.

  Audience:   Just for me
  Output format: Checklist

  Constraints:
    - gym session is 1 hour and works best in the morning
    - grocery shopping takes 45 minutes and must happen after 3 PM
      (store is not crowded before then)
    - leave a 30-minute buffer before 8 PM

  Success criteria:
    - Everything fits with realistic time gaps
    - Shows start and end time for each item

SLM PATH — Chat message:
  The three work tasks are: write a project report (2 hours), reply to emails
  (30 minutes), and attend a video call at 2 PM (1 hour). Please make the schedule.

  Follow-up options:
    Move grocery shopping to 4 PM instead.
    I also need to cook dinner (45 minutes). Where does it fit?
    Show the same schedule as a simple table with start time, task, duration.

LLM PATH — Chat message:
  Help me schedule my day. I need to:
    - Gym: 1 hour (best in the morning)
    - Write a project report: 2 hours
    - Replies to emails: 30 minutes
    - Video call at 2 PM: 1 hour
    - Grocery shopping: 45 minutes (must be after 3 PM)

  Everything must be done by 8 PM. Leave a 30-minute buffer. Show start and
  end times for each item.

  Follow-up options:
    I also need to cook dinner (45 minutes). Fit it in.
    Show this as a simple table.
